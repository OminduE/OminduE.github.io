Screen Deck Safety Access Improvements

This project addressed maintenance access limitations on SS-01 by reviewing the existing three-door arrangement and exploring a safer, more usable access solution. Existing access was difficult because one door was on the top deck and another was blocked by the platform in front of the door. Proposed solutions included bi-fold doors and revised platform and stair configurations to improve access for ongoing maintenance work.

Conveyor Platform Reinforcement

This project addressed an inadequate support condition on the SC-03 conveyor catwalk. Structural review identified that the walkway could not support the required design load, which led to a reinforcement proposal and a new travel path arrangement. Additional modifications were also considered to improve maintenance access to belt scrapers, millwright work areas, and the motor and head-end of the conveyor.

Capstone Project | Hermes VTOL Relay UAV

Hermes was developed as a quadcopter VTOL fixed-wing relay UAV concept intended to support search and rescue operations in remote or obstructed terrain. Rather than replacing an existing search drone, Hermes was designed as a support platform that could help extend communication and operational range.

The project evolved through concept selection, CAD development, prototype assembly, testing, and redesign. Early iterations exposed lift and airflow issues, which led to major changes in the wing extender system and motor placement. Final testing demonstrated stable and reliable quadcopter flight, while fixed-wing transition validation remained limited by rear propulsion issues and timeline constraints.

Project focus

  • Extend communication range in remote search and rescue terrain
  • Support existing UAV operations instead of replacing them

  • Develop a feasible capstone prototype within realistic constraints

Key outcomes

  • VTOL deployment concept aligned well with client needs
  • Testing and redesign improved quadcopter stability
  • Relay payload integration concept proved feasible
  • Future work remains in transition validation and propulsion refinement

Reflection

Across both capstone and co-op work, one of the biggest lessons I learned is that good engineering rarely comes from getting everything right the first time. The strongest outcomes usually came from testing, finding what was not working, and then improving the design in a focused way.

My co-op experience especially showed me that engineering decisions matter most when they can actually be built, maintained, and communicated clearly to the people using them. That made me much more aware of the relationship between technical design, practical constraints, and stakeholder needs.

Going forward, I want to continue working in environments where I can combine CAD, design thinking, hands-on problem solving, and real implementation. That combination is what I find most meaningful and where I believe I do my best work.
